Basic Rules

Gameplay Overview
  • Players work to build their tribes as large as possible by hiring mercenaries, scaveninging junk piles, and winning skirmishes against other players' tribes.
  • Each tribe member card in a tribe represents the number of people shown on the card.
  • The player with the largest tribe (most people) at the end of the game is the winner.
Setup
  • Each player takes an identical starter deck: 4 Refugees, 3 Scavengers, 1 Brawler, 1 Spear, 1 Shovel.
  • Return any additional Refugee cards to the box; they will not be used this game.
  • Sort the remaining cards into three categories: Contested Resources, Junkyard, and Mercenaries.
  • Each player shuffles their deck of 10 cards and places it face down in their play area.
  • Randomly select a player and hand them the initiator card; this player will serve as the initator for the first round.
Key Concepts
  1. A player may take multiple actions in a round.
  2. A player may only perform a given action (dig/hunt/draw/hire/etc.) once per round.
  3. A player may use multiple cards to increase the potency of an action.
  4. Once a player has used a card to perform an action, they may not perform another action with that same card.
  5. A player must combine cards like tools, Group Leaders, and any card with a modifier (e.g. +1, +2, +4) with another card that has the base ability.
  6. A player may only use one tool card for any tribe member, even if that tribe member represents multiple people.
  7. When a player needs to draw more cards than are available in their deck, they first draw all cards from their deck, then shuffles their disscard pile to create a new deck and continues drawing from there.
Game Phases
1. Drawing Phase
  • Discard all cards from the previous round.
  • Each player draw 5 cards from the top of their deck, shuffling their discard pile and adding it to their deck whenever necessary.
  • Beginning with the third round and continuing for the remainder of the game, the Initiator peeks at the top card on the Contested Resources pile and then returns it to the pile.
2. Resource Gathering Phase
  • The Initiator takes the first action and uses any number of cards to gather resources.
  • The Initiator announces how many of their cards will remain for the skirmish.
  • Play continue with the other players, in clockwise order, and they execute the same two steps as above.
3. Skirmish Phase
  • Once all players have taken their resource gathering actions, players reveal their remaining cards for the skirmish and a winner is determined.
  • The winner of the skirmish takes the top card from the Contested Resources pile and adds it to their discard pile.
  • The Initiator passes the initiator card to the next player in clockwise order, starting a new round

Solo Rules

Setup
  • Shuffle all 28 Contested Resource cards together along with 2 Rifles and 2 Thugs. This should form a single pile of 32 cards.
  • Break the 32 cards up as follows into 7 face-down piles, each representing a skirmish that you must encounter:
    • One set of 3 cards.
    • Two sets of 4 cards.
    • Three sets of 5 cards.
    • One set of 6 cards.
  • Select 10 stacks of mercenaries and lay them out in front of you like normal.
  • You may play with or without a Tribal Leader (either way, do not select one for the opponents).
Gameplay
  • There is no concept of an Initator.
  • Every time you shuffle your discard to form a new deck, you must take the top card of your newly shufled deck and remove it permanently from play. This represents the attrition of your tribe over time.
  • There is no forced skirmish phase. On any turn, you may choose to iniate a skirmish as follows:
    • Select one of the available opponents (represented by the face-down stacks of cards).
    • Send as many or as few forces from your hand as you choose.
    • During skirmish resolution, assume the opponent will take the most favorable action.
    • If you win, take one card of your choice from the opponent's skirmish forces as a prize and place it in your discard pile.
    • If you lose, you must permanently remove one card of your choice from your skirmish force and set it aside.
    • Whether you win or lose, the opponent's remaining skirmish force should be removed from play (thus, each of the seven forces are encountered only once).
  • Play continues until you have skirmished with each of the 7 opposing forces.
  • Although there is no set timer for the game, you must constnatly balance the loss of tribe asssets as you cycle through your deck with the risk of losing by going into a skirmish half-cocked.
  • After you fight in the seventh battle, proceed to scoring.
Scoring
  • Contested Resource cards in your tribe count as 2 points.
  • All other cards in your tribe count as 1 point.
  • Keep track of your points from game to game and try to beat your personal best.
Difficulty Level
  • You can always increase the solo difficulty level by adding in more mercenaries with fight or even snipe and removing Tribal Families (which dilute the opposition fight scores).

2-Player Rules

Setup
  • Remove two cards of each kind from the Junkyard.
Other Changes
  • Eliminate all Contested Resources peeking.
  • The number of tribe members a player brings to a skirmish is no longer relevant.
  • Skirmishes must be won by 2. If not, then it is a tie and the players place the Contested Resource on top of the Junkyard and then shuffle the Junkyard.

Recon Module #1 Rules

Module Overview
Recon includes many mercenaries, several of which are designed to work together to produce a web of intelligence and deception.
Setup
  • Put the full stack of Scouting Refugees in play as a separate stack available for hire, but visually separate from the other mercenaries.
  • Select the Spy and 9 other mercenries available for hire for a total of 10 mercenary stacks, plus the Scouting Refugees.
  • Shuffle the 4 Binoculars into the Junkyard.
Details
Spy (tribe member)
  • Grants a recon action:
    • Peek at up to four cards from one player. Choose the player and the target (building, deck, hand, skirmish force).
    • If there are more cards available than four, the spying player must select which cards to view in advance (randomly choosing those cards if needed).
    • After completing your spying action, discard the spy and draw 1 card.
  • In a given round, you may use the standard actions or the spying action, but not both.
  • The Spy's action can be canceled (such as by a Scouting Refugee):
    • The card that cancels a Spy can be revealed from committed cards, hand, or Bunker, regardless of what was being spied upon.
    • If a Spy is canceled, it is still discarded, and the draw of 1 is still performed by the spying player.
  • A Spy can be Sniped. Discard the Spy and draw 1 card.
Scouting Refugee (tribe member)
  • Serves as a Refugee for any actions or game conditions that depend on a refugee card.
  • Two basic uses:
    • Can modify another tribe member's actions, but cannot initate such actions.
    • Alternatively, can be played to cancel a recon action (i.e. Spy, Binoculars, or Scout Tower) by alerting the tribe that the spying activity is occurring. If this special action is performed, discard the Scouting Refugee and draw one card from teh top of your deck.
  • A given Scouting Refugee can only cancel one action, after which it is discaded and then replaced (draw of 1).
Binoculars (instant)
  • Standard actions:
    • Useful in hunting, Binoculars help you plan your actions.
    • An instant action. Does not need to be equipped and thus does not count toward a tribe member's equipement limit of one.
    • Cannot be discarded through a disarm action.
  • Special – grants a recon action:
    • Peek at the top card of every pile in play, returning each card ot the top of its respective pile.
    • Only you may see cards as a result of your recon action. You may not share this information with any other player.
    • Alternatively, the Binoculars can improve any recon action by an additional two cards. Thus, two Binoculars would allow you to see the top 3 cards of each pile, or a Spy with Binoculars would allow you to see up to 6 cards from one player.
    • No tribe member is required in order to use the card in this way.
    • This action cannot be interrupted by a disarm action, but it can be canceled.
  • In a given round, you may use the standard action or the recon action, but not both.
  • As with any card, Binoculars are discarded after use.

Recon Module #2 Rules

Module Overview
This module introduces a couple of the deception elements of Recon. The resolution of these cards during the skirmish can sometimes get confusing. It is important to remember that resolution decisions happen in play order (starting with that round's Initiator) rather than in real-time.
Setup
  • Put the Assassin, Guard, Provocateur, and Rogue in play together as mercenaries available for hire.
  • Select 6 other mercenaries for a total of 10 stacks.
Details
Assassin (tribe member)
  • Like the Sniper Team, this mercenary has the ability to snipe an opponent's tribe members during resource gathering or the skirmish.
  • Unlike the Sniper Team, the Assassin is restricted to only attacking single-unit tribe members.
Guard (tribe member)
  • An inexpensive fighter, similar to a Brawler.
  • Can be used to cancel any action outside of the skirmish that targets your card(s). Examples include spying, sniping, or disarming while you gather resources, attacks against your buildings, etc.
  • If you do use a Guard for its cancel ability, discard it and draw a new card from the top of your deck.
Provocateur (tribe member)
  • Standard draw/fight actions.
  • Special – grants a special means of fighting through intimidation:
    • During your turn of the skirmish resolution phase, you may announce an intent to use the number of tribe members you brought to the skirmish as your fight score.
    • Count the total size of your force (tribe members you brought, even if they are not fighters) ignoring any tools or other combat modifiers, but including leader and building abilities to increase the size of your force.
    • Use the total force size as your fight score.
    • If your Provocateur gets sniped, your fight socre reverts back to being calculated in the usual manner.
    • The Gangster leader ability does not count towards the Provocateur ability.
  • A player in later turn order can snipe a Provocateur in earlier turn order.
Rogue (tribe member)
  • Standard draw/fight actions.
  • Special – grants a special skirmish win condition:
    • If a skirmish ends with a tie for highest fight score, the first player in turn order with a Rogue in the skirmish automatically wins the Contested Resource.
    • Only a tie for highest fight score is considered, not the number of tribe members. The Rogue ability constitutes a higher order of precedence than the number of tirbe members brought to the skirmish.
    • It is not necessary that the player using the Rogue be involved in the tie. Any two players with identical fight scores trigger the potential for a Rogue win condition for any player in the skirmish. At this point, the number of tribe members in the skirmish is not relevant.
    • Teh Rogue's ability to steal the Contested Resource is passive; you do not need to give up the Rogue's fight in order to steal it.
  • Your Rogue can be targed by a snipe action.
  • Only the first Rogue action can be successful per round (resolved in turn order).
  • A player in lower turn order can snipe a Rogue in higher turn order.

Recon Module #3 Rules

Module Overview
This module introduces the remaining mercenaries from Recon as well as two new buildings.
Setup
  • Put the Courier, Drill Sergeant, Scrappers, and Tinker in play together as mercenaries available for hire.
  • Select 6 other mercenaries for a total of 10 stacks.
  • Add the Schematics Pile (from HQ) and the two new buildings (Portable Decoys and Scout Tower).
  • Finally, substitute Hardy Scavengers in place of the standard Scavengers during player setup.
Details
Courier (tribe member)
  • Has a powerful draw ability.
  • When you perform a draw using a Courier, you must discard two of the drawn cards.
  • Even if other cards are used to augment the draw ability, only two of the drawn cards must be discarded per Courier played.
Drill Sergeant (tribe member)
  • Standard dig/fight actions.
  • Special – retrieve two cards from the discard pile, shuffle them into your deck, and then draw the top two cards from your deck.
  • This draw is distinct from the standard draw action.
  • This draw is not modifiable.
  • If you have no cards in your discard pile, you still draw two.
  • If you have no cards in your deck, then both cards retreived from the discard pile will form your new deck.
  • Using the Drill Sergeant in the skirmish will not permit you to add new cards to your skirmish force.
Hardy Scavenger (tribe member)
  • Similar to a standard Scavenger, the Hardy Scavenger is a jack-of-all-trades, master-of-none. Unlike the standard unit, the Hardy Scavenger has 0 for draw and fight. Thus, modifers are required to make those actions useful.
  • The Hardy Scavenger has a special ability that triggers only at the end of a skirmish in which the player does not win. If you send a Hardy Scavenger into the skirmish and you either lose or tie, then any Hardy Scavengers you sent are returned to your hand at the end of the round.
  • Even if one or more Hardy Scavengers return to your hand, you still perform a standard draw action at the start of the next round.
Scrappers (tribe member)
  • When you perform a dig action using Scrappers, you have choices to make:
    • You may perform your dig from the top or bottom of the pile.
    • When you return cards to the pile after digging, you may choose to return cards to the top or bottom of the pile.
  • You still only retrieve one card from the set of cards you collect.
  • When you return cards, you may choose to return some to the top and others to the bottom.
Tinker (tribe member)
  • A lighter-weight version of the Engineer.
  • The tinker permits you to dig in the Engineering Schematics pile. However, with only a digging modifier, you will need to pair up with another tribe member that has a base dig ability.
  • Any tribe member with a base dig ability (even a standard Refugee) can team up with a Tinker to dig in the Engineering Schematics pile.
  • Tinkers do not contribute to the tie breaker when competing for the The Masons gang (ties are broken by number of Engineers).
  • Note: when playing with the Tinker, you may find it helpful to add in the Toolkit to the Junkyard.
Portable Decoys (building)
  • Increases the size of your force in the skirmish for the purpose of tie-breaking or leveraging special abilities.
  • For every 2 cards you bring to the skirmish, increase the effective size of your skirmish force by 1 (round down, no half credit).
Scout Tower (building)
  • Discard 1 card to activate your tower.
  • Select any two piles as your target.
  • Peek at the first two cards for each target and return them in their original order.

Recon Module #4 Rules

Module Overview
This module deals exclusively with the Contested Resources pile, and thus can easiy be combined with any other module.
Setup
  • Shuffle all 28 Contested Resources (the 14 original plus the 14 new in Recon) together into one draw pile; then, draw the top 14 and use those for the game.
  • Alternatively, choose 1 copy of each Contested Resource (7 from the base game and 7 from Recon).
Details
Cargo Sled (tool)
  • Standard dig/hunt actions.
  • The special ability kicks in when digging. You have the option of keeping an additional tool or medicine from the set of cards you retrive from the Junkyard.
Combat Medics (tribe member)
  • Carries medicine like a Medic.
  • Can save against a snipe attack and fight in the same round.
Demolition Team (tribe member)
  • Standard dig/fight actions.
  • Alternately, their special ability can be used to reset the build timer on one under-construction building. Afterwards, draw two cards.
Tear Gas (instant)
  • Used only in the skirmish.
  • This card's use is declared on your turn within the skirmish phase.
  • It does not need to be equipped by a tribe member and cannot be disarmed.
  • When a player uses Tear Gas, one opposing trib emember is targeted. The targeted tribe member's fight score (or fight modifier) is set to 0.
  • This is not a snipe attack and thus cannot be blocked by a save action.
  • Tear Gas is not effective against a Provocateur (cumulative tribe member count is used).
  • Tear Gas can indirectly counter a Rogue by breaking tied skirmish.
Tribal Hunters (tribe member)
  • Just like the Tribe Family, comes in a 2-prson, 3-person, and 4-person variety.
  • Unlike the Tribe Family, these groups are dedicated hunters and have a minor fight strength.

Recon Module #5 Rules

Module Overview
This module introduces the remaining content of the Recon expansion.
Details
Humanitarians (gang)
  • Select a full stack of 20 Refugee cards and make them available for hire in addition to the 10 mercenary stacks you choose to select.
  • Awarded based on the number of cards with the "refugee" keyword in the title (including those you start with, find in the Junkyard, and hire).
  • There is no tie-breaker. In the event of a tie, this gang is not awarded.
Map (instant)
  • An instant action. Does not need to be equipped and thus does not count toward a tribe member's one-card equipment limit.
  • Cannot be discarded through a disarm action.
  • Play this card to retrieve a tribe member you have just used and apply them to another action this same round.
  • Does not permit an action to be performed more than once.
The Swindler (leader)
  • Discard one Refugee card to reduce the cost of hiring by 1 food or discard 2 Refugees to reduce the cost of hiring by 1 medicine.
  • This special action can only be performed once per turn.
The Yardmaster (leader)
  • Once per round, you may peek at the top two cards of the Junkyard. Return both cards to the top or bottom of the pile (preserving the original card order).
  • This action may be taken at any time during the round.
  • This action cannot be canceled or otherwise interrupted.
  • This action is not modifiable.

Variants

Leader Drafting
To incorporate leader acquisition into the game and infuse more choice, this variant allows for drafting leaders during the game rather than at the beginning:
  • Shuffle all the leader cards together and create a new face-down pile next to the other piles.
  • During the game, players may use the hire action to persuade a leader to join their tribe by bribing them with food:
    • Food (from hunting, buildings, leader powers, etc.) is used to bribe new leaders. For each food generated, the player may draw 1 card from the top of the leaders pile.
    • A player may not hire more than once per turn. Thus, if you hire a leader, you cannot hire a mercenary that same round.
    • The player may take one of the leader cards drawn in this manner and return the others to the bottom of the pile.
    • The leader card is then placed face-up in front of the player.
    • All leader powers on this card are immediately in effect.
    • Actions that permit peeking at a pile can target the leaders pile as well.
  • At any point during the game, a player may choose to perform this action, even if they have already taken a leader. When a new set of leaders is drawn in this manner, the player may choose to take one and replace their current leader (returning the rest to the bottom of the leader pile) or return all such cards to the bottom of the pile.
Alternate Starting Hands
For variety, you may want to experiment with different starting hands. Suggested substitutions are as follows:
  • Scouting Refugees for Refugees (depending on what leaders you have, this can be challenging as you will find a large number of card modifiers rather than base abilities).
  • Hardy Scavengers for Scavengers.
  • Guards for Brawlers.
Note: with the greater variety of mercenaries offered through the Recon expansion, it is recommended that you not include any mercenaries for hire that are part of the players' starting hands.
