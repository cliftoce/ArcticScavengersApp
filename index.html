Basic Rules

Gameplay Overview
  • Players work to build their tribes as large as possible by hiring mercenaries, scaveninging junk piles, and winning skirmishes against other players' tribes.
  • Each tribe member card in a tribe represents the number of people shown on the card.
  • The player with the largest tribe (most people) at the end of the game is the winner.
Setup
  • Each player takes an identical starter deck: 4 Refugees, 3 Scavengers, 1 Brawler, 1 Spear, 1 Shovel.
  • Return any additional Refugee cards to the box; they will not be used this game.
  • Sort the remaining cards into three categories: Contested Resources, Junkyard, and Mercenaries.
  • Each player shuffles their deck of 10 cards and places it face down in their play area.
  • Randomly select a player and hand them the initiator card; this player will serve as the initator for the first round.
Game Phases
1. Drawing Phase
  • Discard all cards from the previous round.
  • Each player draw 5 cards from the top of their deck, shuffling their discard pile and adding it to their deck whenever necessary.
  • Beginning with the third round and continuing for the remainder of the game, the Initiator peeks at the top card on the Contested Resources pile and then returns it to the pile.
2. Resource Gathering Phase
  • The Initiator takes the first action and uses any number of cards to gather resources.
  • The Initiator announces how many of their cards will remain for the skirmish.
  • Play continue with the other players, in clockwise order, and they execute the same two steps as above.
3. Skirmish Phase
  • Once all players have taken their resource gathering actions, players reveal their remaining cards for the skirmish and a winner is determined.
  • The winner of the skirmish takes the top card from the Contested Resources pile and adds it to their discard pile.
  • The Initiator passes the initiator card to the next player in clockwise order, starting a new round

Solo Rules

Setup
  • Shuffle all 28 Contested Resource cards together along with 2 Rifles and 2 Thugs. This should form a single pile of 32 cards.
  • Break the 32 cards up as follows into 7 face-down piles, each representing a skirmish that you must encounter:
    • One set of 3 cards.
    • Two sets of 4 cards.
    • Three sets of 5 cards.
    • One set of 6 cards.
  • Select 10 stacks of mercenaries and lay them out in front of you like normal.
  • You may play with or without a Tribal Leader (either way, do not select one for the opponents).
Gameplay
  • There is no concept of an Initator.
  • Every time you shuffle your discard to form a new deck, you must take the top card of your newly shufled deck and remove it permanently from play. This represents the attrition of your tribe over time.
  • There is no forced skirmish phase. On any turn, you may choose to iniate a skirmish as follows:
    • Select one of the available opponents (represented by the face-down stacks of cards).
    • Send as many or as few forces from your hand as you choose.
    • During skirmish resolution, assume the opponent will take the most favorable action.
    • If you win, take one card of your choice from the opponent's skirmish forces as a prize and place it in your discard pile.
    • If you lose, you must permanently remove one card of your choice from your skirmish force and set it aside.
    • Whether you win or lose, the opponent's remaining skirmish force should be removed from play (thus, each of the seven forces are encountered only once).
  • Play continues until you have skirmished with each of the 7 opposing forces.
  • Although there is no set timer for the game, you must constnatly balance the loss of tribe asssets as you cycle through your deck with the risk of losing by going into a skirmish half-cocked.
  • After you fight in the seventh battle, proceed to scoring.
Scoring
  • Contested Resource cards in your tribe count as 2 points.
  • All other cards in your tribe count as 1 point.
  • Keep track of your points from game to game and try to beat your personal best.
Difficulty Level
  • You can always increase the solo difficulty level by adding in more mercenaries with fight or even snipe and removing Tribal Families (which dilute the opposition fight scores).

2-Player Rules

Setup
  • Remove two cards of each kind from the Junkyard.
Other Changes
  • Eliminate all Contested Resources peeking.
  • The number of tribe members a player brings to a skirmish is no longer relevant.
  • Skirmishes must be won by 2. If not, then it is a tie and the players place the Contested Resource on top of the Junkyard and then shuffle the Junkyard.

HQ Module #1 Details

Setup
  • Add the stack of Medic cards alongside the other mercenary cards.
  • Shuffle the 8 new Junkyard cards into the Junkyard deck. For now ignore the special ability of the Toolkit.
  • Locate the Gearheads and Pharmers. Place them on the table next to the mercenary stacks.

HQ Module #2 Details

Setup
  • Set up the game just as you did for HQ Module #1.
  • Add the stack of Engineer cards alongside the other mercenary cards.
  • Shuffle the 12 building cards together (indicated by the building icon in the upper left corner) and place them face-down next to the Junkyard pile (they form a third pile of cards next to the Junkyard and Contested Resources.
  • Add the 3rd gang card (Masons) next to the other 2 gangs.
  • Players can now use the "special ability" from the Toolkit (since it works with buildings).

HQ Module #3 Details

Setup
  • Set up the game just as you did for HQ Module #2.
  • Shuffle the tribal leader cards together and deal two, face-down, to each player. From those two, each player keeps only 1 to represent their tribal leader (players may look at them to decide). The card is kept face-up in the player's play area for everyone to see. The other card is set aside and will not be used this game.

HQ Module #4 Details

Setup
  • Set up the game just as you did for HQ Module #3.
  • Add two new rules:
    • A Saboteur may attack a building that is completed and disable it.
    • A Sniper Team may attack a tribal leader, wounding him and removing his advantage until a med is applied to the leader to heal him.

Recon Module #1 Details

Overview
Recon includes many mercenaries, several of which are designed to work together to produce a web of intelligence and deception.
Setup
  • Put the full stack of Scouting Refugees in play as a separate stack available for hire, but visually separate from the other mercenaries.
  • Select the Spy and 9 other mercenries available for hire for a total of 10 mercenary stacks, plus the Scouting Refugees.
  • Shuffle the 4 Binoculars into the Junkyard.

Recon Module #2 Details

Overview
This module introduces a couple of the deception elements of Recon. The resolution of these cards during the skirmish can sometimes get confusing. It is important to remember that resolution decisions happen in play order (starting with that round's Initiator) rather than in real-time.
Setup
  • Put the Assassin, Guard, Provocateur, and Rogue in play together as mercenaries available for hire.
  • Select 6 other mercenaries for a total of 10 stacks.

Recon Module #3 Details

Overview
This module introduces the remaining mercenaries from Recon as well as two new buildings.
Setup
  • Put the Courier, Drill Sergeant, Scrappers, and Tinker in play together as mercenaries available for hire.
  • Select 6 other mercenaries for a total of 10 stacks.
  • Add the Schematics Pile (from HQ) and the two new buildings (Portable Decoys and Scout Tower).
  • Finally, substitute Hardy Scavengers in place of the standard Scavengers during player setup.

Recon Module #4 Details

Overview
This module deals exclusively with the Contested Resources pile, and thus can easiy be combined with any other module.
Setup
  • Shuffle all 28 Contested Resources (the 14 original plus the 14 new in Recon) together into one draw pile; then, draw the top 14 and use those for the game.
  • Alternatively, choose 1 copy of each Contested Resource (7 from the base game and 7 from Recon).

Recon Module #5 Details

Overview
This module introduces the remaining content of the Recon expansion.
